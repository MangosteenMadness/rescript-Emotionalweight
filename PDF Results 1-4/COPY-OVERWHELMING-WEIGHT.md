## PAGE 6-7: YOUR 5 HEALING PROMPTS

**Header:**
Prompts for Beginning the Work (With Support)

**Subheader:**
These prompts are starting points, not complete solutions. Ideally, work through these with a therapist or trusted support person. If you do them alone, have crisis resources ready and give yourself permission to stop if it becomes too much.

**Important:** Healing is not linear. You don't have to do all of these. You don't have to do them perfectly. You just have to start.

---

**Prompt 1 Box (Light gray background, rounded corners):**
**1. What does this pain feel like in my body, and where is it stored?**

Trauma lives in the body. Where do you feel it? Your chest, throat, stomach, shoulders?

Write about: The physical sensation of your pain. Give it a color, temperature, texture. Don't try to make it make sense. Just describe the sensation. This begins the process of reconnecting with your body.

*Note: If this brings up overwhelming physical sensations, stop and try grounding (5 things you see, 4 you hear, 3 you touch, 2 you smell, 1 you taste).*

---

**Prompt 2 Box:**
**2. What story am I telling myself about why I deserve this burden?**

Often, we carry shame alongside pain. We tell ourselves it's our fault, we deserved it, or we're uniquely unlovable.

Write about: The narrative you've internalized. What do you believe about yourself because of what happened? Write it down, no matter how painful. Then ask: "Is this objectively true, or is this what trauma taught me?"

*This prompt challenges core beliefs. It might be the hardest one.*

---

**Prompt 3 Box:**
**3. What would it feel like to be unburdened, even for one day?**

You might not be able to imagine full healing yet. That's okay. What would one day of relief feel like?

Write about: One day where you wake up and the weight is gone. What would you do? How would you move through the world? What small joy would you notice? Let yourself imagine this, even if you don't believe it's possible.

---

**Prompt 4 Box:**
**4. Who or what am I holding onto that I need to release?**

Sometimes we hold onto pain because letting go feels like betrayal, or because the pain is the last connection we have to someone or something.

Write about: What you're gripping. Is it a person, a belief, an identity, a sense of loyalty? What would happen if you loosened your grip? What are you afraid you'd lose?

---

**Prompt 5 Box:**
**5. What support do I need to ask for that I've been avoiding?**

You've been doing this alone. What help have you needed but been too afraid, ashamed, or exhausted to ask for?

Write about: What you actually need (not what you think you should need). Money? Housing? Therapy? Someone to watch your kids while you rest? Medical care? It's okay to need things. Write down what you need, even if you don't know how you'll get it yet.

## PAGE 8: THE 4-DAY RESCRIPT PROTOCOL

**Header:**
A Tool to Begin (But Not a Complete Solution)

**Subheader:**
The 4-day protocol can provide significant relief, especially when paired with professional support. Many people with complex trauma find that doing this protocol *with* a therapist is most effective.

**Before You Begin:**
‚úì Tell someone you trust that you're doing this
‚úì Have crisis resources saved in your phone
‚úì Set up supports (comfort items, grounding tools)
‚úì Give yourself permission to stop if it's too much
‚úì Consider working with a therapist through the process

---

**Day 1 Box (Light purple background):**
**üìù Day 1: Expressive Writing**
Choose one experience to focus on (not everything at once). Write for 20 minutes. Be as raw as you need to be. If you dissociate, stop and ground yourself first. 

*You don't have to start with the worst thing. Start with something painful but manageable.*

---

**Day 2 Box (Light blue background):**
**üîÑ Day 2: Perspective Shift**
Read what you wrote yesterday (if you can). Then write about the same experience from the perspective of someone who loves you. What would they say happened? What would they want you to know?

*This is about adding complexity, not minimizing your experience.*

---

**Day 3 Box (Light teal background):**
**üí° Day 3: Meaning Making**
This day is optional if it feels too hard. If you do it: What did surviving this cost you? What did it teach you about the world? About people? About yourself? Is there any strength you developed that you didn't have before?

*There's no pressure to find silver linings. Sometimes the only meaning is "I endured."*

---

**Day 4 Box (Light green background):**
**üåÖ Day 4: Integration**
Write a letter from your future self ‚Äî the version of you that has found a way to live with this without it consuming you. What does that person want you to know? What advice do they have?

*You're not imagining being "cured." You're imagining being able to carry it differently.*
